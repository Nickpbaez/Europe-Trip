Evening
Széchenyi Baths — the iconic one. Go after 7 PM: fewer crowds, domes lit up, warm water against cool night air. The quintessential Budapest experience.
Daytime
Gellért Baths — Art Nouveau palace of a bathhouse. More ornate and photogenic than Széchenyi, less of a party.
Any day
Rudas Baths — 16th-century Ottoman bath with a rooftop hot tub overlooking the Danube. Go for the panoramic pool.
Afternoon
Palatinus (Margaret Island) — outdoor summer pools, slides, wave pool, lángos stands. Best on a hot afternoon.
From noon
Szimpla Kert — the original ruin bar (Kazinczy u. 14). Former factory packed with art, bathtubs, hanging bikes. Quiet & photogenic in the afternoon, party after 10 PM. Free entry.
11 PM+
Instant-Fogas — mega-venue, two former ruin bars fused across multiple floors with 18 bars. The heart of the Jewish Quarter party scene.
Night
Mika Tivadar Mulató / 400 Bar / Kocka — the cluster of garden clubs around Kazinczy Street. Bar-hop between them.
Sunset
360 Bar — rooftop on Andrássy Ave with 360° city views, cocktails, DJ nights. Arrive before 6 PM for a sunset seat. Smart casual.
Fri / Sat night
Széchenyi "Sparty" — after-dark thermal bath party. Touristy but a only-in-Budapest novelty if you're up for it.
Morning
Buda Castle & Castle District — cobbled streets, royal palace, quiet and romantic. Take the funicular up or walk.
Any time
Fisherman's Bastion — fairytale terraces with the best panoramic view over the Danube and Parliament. Free; go early or at golden hour.
Day
Hungarian Parliament — the riverfront icon. Book a guided interior tour ahead, or just admire it from the Pest embankment.
Evening
Chain Bridge + Danube walk — stroll across at night when everything's lit. Pairs perfectly with a sunset rooftop drink.
Fri–Sun
Liberty Bridge "picnic" — in summer the bridge closes to traffic and locals hang out on the ironwork. Spontaneous, free, very local.
Half day
Margaret Island — green car-free island in the Danube. Rent a bike, see the musical fountain, escape the city heat.
Day
Great Market Hall — vast 19th-century market hall. Lángos upstairs, paprika and Tokaji wine downstairs. Best lunch-time browse.
Anytime
Try: lángos, goulash, chimney cake — the essential Hungarian street eats. Lángos at the baths or market, goulash at any traditional étterem.
Sun 9 AM–2 PM
Szimpla Sunday Farmers' Market — the ruin bar turns into a farmers' market: organic cheese, honey, bread, craft spirits. (Note: you leave Tue, so only if plans shift.)
Evening
Fröccs & Pálinka — wine spritzer (fröccs) and fruit brandy (pálinka) are the local pours. Start a night out with one in the Jewish Quarter.
